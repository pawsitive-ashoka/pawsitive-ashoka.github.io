On-Ground & Feeding

The heart of Pawsitive. Present every single day.

what we do

This team oversees year-round feeding operations for dogs within the club’s jurisdiction, coordinating student schedules during the semester and ensuring continuity of care during vacation periods. They respond to medical emergencies involving dogs and cats, maintain stocked medical kits, and ensure medications are administered on schedule. The team also keeps an updated census of animals under care and coordinates veterinary visits and follow-ups as needed.

responsibilities

  • Year-round feeding operations twice daily, coordinating student schedules including during vacation periods
  • Responding to medical emergencies — first assessment, contacting vets, transporting animals if needed
  • Maintaining stocked first-aid kits and medication schedules for all animals under care
  • Maintaining an updated census of all animals — names, health records, feeding notes, and status
  • Coordinating all veterinary visits, follow-up appointments, and post-treatment care
  • Monitoring behavioural and health changes in campus animals and flagging concerns early

past highlights

Blanket Drive

As winter temperatures in Sonipat dropped to single digits, Pawsitive organized a Blanket Drive to help campus dogs withstand the cold. Warm blankets were distributed across campus to protect the dogs during harsh winter nights.

Collar Drive

Pawsitive organized a Reflective Collar Drive to improve the safety of campus dogs at night. Reflective collars now increase the dogs' visibility to drivers in low-light conditions, reducing their vulnerability to accidents.

Daily Feeding Drives

Pawsitive feeds campus dogs twice daily — afternoon and night — using nutritious leftover meals from Ashoka University's dining facilities. This reduces food wastage while ensuring the dogs receive a balanced, reliable diet.

Medication Management and Treatment Monitoring

When a campus dog is prescribed medication, Pawsitive volunteers coordinate to administer the correct dosage at the right intervals. The team closely monitors each treatment routine to ensure effective and timely recovery.

Sterilization Drive

Pawsitive regularly organizes sterilization drives to support the long-term health of campus dogs and promote responsible population management. Sterilization reduces uncontrolled population growth, related injuries, and health risks for individual animals.

Tick Removal

Pawsitive periodically conducts tick removal drives to protect campus dogs from parasites that can cause skin irritation, weakness, and serious blood-borne infections. Volunteers apply spot-on anti-tick treatments directly to the dogs' skin.

Vaccination Drive

Pawsitive conducts regular vaccination drives to protect campus dogs from common and life-threatening diseases. Puppies receive multi-dose vaccines in early months, while adult dogs get annual boosters to maintain immunity.

Visits to the Veterinary Clinic

Pawsitive ensures campus dogs receive timely veterinary care whenever needed. For serious conditions or emergencies, the club arranges visits to larger hospitals equipped for advanced procedures, including surgeries and specialized treatment.
