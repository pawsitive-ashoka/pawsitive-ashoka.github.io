🐾 About Us

Pawsitive is Ashoka University’s student-led animal welfare club, established in 2018, and dedicated to the care and well-being of the street dogs and cats within and around the campus. Driven by a community of animal welfare advocates, we work to alleviate suffering through veterinary care, sterilization, vaccination, and tick removal drives, while also fostering awareness about animal health and responsible pet ownership. Through collaboration, education, and hands-on action, Pawsitive strives to build a culture of compassion, empathy, and coexistence between animals and the university community.

our work

what we do

We organize veterinary drives, sterilization and vaccination programs, tick removal sessions, and campus awareness events — all aimed at improving animal health and promoting responsible coexistence.

our story

how it all began

Pawsitive was born from a small group of students who noticed the campus dogs needed more consistent care. What started as informal feeding rounds grew into a full-fledged welfare club, with proper vet tie-ups, volunteer rosters, and a whole lot of love.

our mission

what drives us

To make Ashoka a safe and loving environment for every animal that calls it home. We believe in coexistence — that a thriving campus community includes its four-legged residents too.

our values

what we believe in

Compassion without conditions. Every dog, every cat, every creature matters — regardless of breed, background, or how scruffy they look. We show up for them, rain or shine.
