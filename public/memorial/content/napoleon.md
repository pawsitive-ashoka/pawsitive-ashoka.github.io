Napoleon was the runt of his litter of five. Unlike his famous namesake, however, he had little interest in charging into conflict when his siblings tumbled into their playful battles. Instead, Napoleon often preferred to watch from the sidelines, occasionally offering a small bark as if cheering them on.

What he loved most, though, was people. He would waddle up to anyone nearby, looking up with big, bright eyes that seemed to ask to be picked up and held. When his wish was granted, his tiny tail would wag with unrestrained joy.
