Ladla was an elderly brown-black dog and the de facto leader of the Gate 1 pack. Despite his position, he was never aggressive or intimidating. Instead, he carried himself with the quiet composure of a gentle old soul, and his eyes held the calm, knowing expression of a dog who had seen many years pass by on campus. Ladla had been around for a long time — long enough to become a familiar and reassuring presence to many who crossed Gate 1.

In his final months, age began to catch up with him. He struggled with a weakened immune system and recurring mange, which gradually took a toll on his health. He is remembered as exactly what he always was: a dignified, unhurried presence who asked for nothing and gave warmth to everyone.
