Koda is quite unlike his bear-like counterpart, Kenai. While Kenai — and most other dogs — are primarily motivated by food, Koda much prefers being the centre of human attention. He is happiest when people are around to admire and fuss over him.

That said, Koda seems blissfully unaware of his own size. In his attempts to show affection, he has a habit of enthusiastically leaning or pushing himself against people, often with far more force than he seems to realise.
